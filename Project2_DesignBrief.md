The game Broken Box is a platform shooter arcade game, single screen but with controls built to resemble classic Mega Man games. The game is intended to be an endless wave battle game, wherein the player fights boxes with various movement mechanics to try to achieve a high score. The core mechanic of this game has a fairly simple two part loop: you get into position to shoot an enemy by moving using the arrow keys, press the spacebar to fire a pellet at the enemy, and then the enemy is defeated, scoring you 10 points. The overall game runs similarly to an arcade cabinet but without the money, with first the title/instructions screen, followed by the play screen, followed by the game over screen if you fall off the platforms or run out of health from running into enemies. Collision was used for many objects, with the four main collision entities being enemies, the player, lemons (your projectile) and platforms, of which the only collisions not implemented are enemy to enemy, lemon to lemon, and player to lemon. All other entities interact about how you would expect, including knockback and invincibility frames for the player when running into an enemy. The main change from the initial idea to the final build was the addition of the firing mechanic, as we had initially started with the idea of a platformer akin to Donkey Kong, but then realized that the ability to shoot lemons like in Mega Man might make the game more interesting.
